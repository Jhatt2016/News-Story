Seattle Teen Survives After Jumping from Aurora Bridge

SEATTLE, WA – A Seattle teenager is recovering after jumping from the Aurora Bridge on Thursday afternoon, authorities confirmed. The incident prompted a rapid emergency response and temporarily disrupted traffic in the area.

According to witnesses, the teen was seen standing on the bridge’s railing before making the leap. Bystanders quickly called 911, and emergency personnel, including the Seattle Fire Department and Seattle Police, arrived on the scene within minutes.

Authorities have not released the teen’s identity but confirmed that the individual was transported to Harborview Medical Center with serious injuries. Officials have not disclosed the teen’s current condition but stated they are alive and receiving treatment.

The Aurora Bridge, officially known as the George Washington Memorial Bridge, has been the site of past incidents, leading to discussions about increasing safety measures. Community advocates continue to push for enhanced barriers and mental health resources for those in crisis.

If you or someone you know is struggling, resources are available. The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline can be reached at 988 for confidential support 24/7.
